## 1. Executive summary — the whole story in 60 seconds

Airbnb proved the world wants to stay in homes, not just hotels. But as it grew to
millions of listings, the *hosting* got lost — surprise fees, slow support,
unreliable hosts, and no real help when a trip goes wrong. A growing number of
travellers now quietly ask, *"why didn't I just book a hotel?"*

**Ryo answers that.** It keeps everything convenient about Airbnb but rebuilds the
experience around three things people actually want: **trust** (every host
verified, the best homes inspected), **service** (a real 24/7 concierge in the
guest's own language), and **honesty** (the price you see is the price you pay).

We make money the same clean way Airbnb does — a small percentage of each
booking — with **no hidden fees and no paid placement**. We launch in India and
Southeast Asia, where travel is growing fast and trust in existing platforms is
low, starting with a small, hand-picked set of excellent homes.

The app is largely **built** — guests can browse, search, book, and manage trips;
hosts can list and manage homes; our team has the tools to keep it safe. What
remains is connecting it to live payments and opening it carefully to the public —
a faster, lower-risk phase than the building that's already behind us.

---

## 2. The problem we're solving

Airbnb scaled the *idea* of home-sharing brilliantly. But scale made the hosting
**transactional**, and four problems became normal:

- **Surprise fees.** A price looks fine in search, then cleaning and service fees pile on at checkout. People feel tricked.
- **Slow, scripted support.** When a key doesn't work or a home isn't as promised, help can take hours or days — and often it's a script, not a solution.
- **Unreliable hosts.** Little real vetting. Last-minute cancellations. Listings that don't match the photos.
- **No real recourse.** When something goes wrong, it becomes a refund argument, not a fix. The guest is left to sort out their own ruined trip.

The result: short-term rentals can feel like a gamble. For a special trip — a
honeymoon, a family holiday, an important work stay — many people retreat to a
hotel just to be safe. **That hesitation is the gap Ryo fills.**

---

## 3. The Ryo solution — three promises

Ryo rebuilds the short-term stay around **service quality**. Three promises sit at
the centre of everything:

| Promise | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Verified hosts** | Every host proves who they are and that they own/manage the property. The best ("Boutique") homes get an on-site inspection before they go live. |
| **24/7 multilingual concierge** | A real human answers within ~2 minutes on chat, in the guest's own language — any hour, anywhere. If something's wrong, they *fix* it, not just log it. |
| **Honest pricing & guarantees** | The total price is shown up front — no checkout surprises. And stays are backed by guarantees: arrival protection, re-booking if a home falls through, refunds when quality fails. |

This is the Japanese idea of *omotenashi* — anticipating a guest's needs before
they're even spoken — turned into an actual product and service.

> **For guests**, Ryo is a stay that feels *prepared for them*.
> **For hosts**, it's a platform that *protects their home and their name.*

## 4. Who Ryo is for

**Guests (the demand side)**
Travellers roughly 28–50, medium-to-high income, who take 3–10 trips a year — a
mix of holidays, "bleisure" (business + leisure), staycations, family trips, and
solo escapes. Most have been let down by Airbnb at least once, and will happily
pay a small premium for a stay they don't have to worry about.

**Hosts (the supply side)**
People who own or run 1–10 quality properties and take hospitality seriously.
They're frustrated by opaque rankings, slow payouts, and risky guests on existing
platforms. Ryo gives them a platform that backs them.

**The concierge team (internal)**
Multilingual staff running 24/7 support. On Ryo, their tools are treated as a
**first-class product**, not a back-office afterthought — because the service
*is* the brand.

## 5. The guest journey — step by step

This is the heart of the product. Here's exactly what a traveller experiences,
in order.

**Step 1 — Arrive & explore.** The guest opens Ryo and lands on a clean grid of
beautiful homes, photos first. A row of **categories** (beachfront, cabins, city
apartments…) and a **search bar** sit at the top. They can browse freely without
signing in.

**Step 2 — Search & filter.** They type a destination and dates, then narrow by
price, number of guests, amenities (pool, wifi, kitchen), and property type. A
**map view** shows where each home sits.

**Step 3 — Look at a home.** Tapping a home opens the **listing page**: a full
photo gallery, the description, amenities, house rules, reviews from past guests,
and the host's profile. A **booking card** stays visible with the nightly price
and total.

**Step 4 — Book the stay.** They pick dates on a **calendar**, see the **full
price broken down** (nightly rate, service fee, total — nothing hidden), and
confirm. *Today this uses a demo payment step — the booking is created for real,
but no money moves yet. Real payments are the next major milestone.*

**Step 5 — Manage the trip.** The booking appears under **Trips**, with details,
address, check-in info, and the ability to **cancel** if plans change.

**Step 6 — Their personal hub.** Every guest has an **Account** dashboard:
upcoming and past trips, saved favourites, messages, and settings (name, language,
currency). They can also keep a **Wishlist** of homes they love but haven't
booked.

> **In short:** Browse → Search → View → Book → Manage → Personal hub. The flow
> people already know from Airbnb — but calmer and more honest at every step.

## 6. The host experience

Hosts get their own professional dashboard to run their business:

- **Dashboard** — a glance at upcoming check-ins, this month's earnings, rating, occupancy.
- **Create a listing** — add a home with photos, description, amenities, pricing, rules. *(Live — saves real data.)*
- **My Listings** — see and edit all their homes in one place.
- **Calendar** — colour-coded view of available, booked, and blocked dates.
- **Bookings** — every reservation with details and a clear payout breakdown.
- **Earnings** — this month, last month, year-to-date, plus the payout queue.
- **Inbox** — guest messages, thread by thread.
- **Reviews** — ratings over time.
- **Settings** — profile, payout details, taxes, and co-host team.

> Some host screens currently show realistic *sample* data so we can demonstrate
> the full experience before the live plumbing is finished. The create-listing
> and my-listings flows already save and load real data.

## 7. The operations (admin) experience

Behind the scenes, our own team needs tools to keep Ryo safe, fair, and running.
This is what makes the "trust" and "guarantee" promises real — there's a team and
a toolset behind every booking.

- **Overview** — the health of the whole platform at a glance.
- **Users & Bookings** — search anyone, inspect any reservation, take action when needed.
- **Moderation** — review listings and flagged content before they cause problems.
- **Incidents** — handle anything that goes wrong during a stay.
- **Finance** — track total sales, reconcile payments, manage payouts and refunds.
- **Audit log** — a permanent record of who did what, for accountability.
- **System health & feature switches** — monitor the platform and turn features on or off safely.

## 8. How it all fits together — the simple version

You don't need the technical details, but here's the shape of it in everyday terms:

- **One product, two faces.** Ryo runs as a **phone app** (iPhone/Android) *and* a **website**. Each screen is built once and works on both — saving time and keeping everything consistent.
- **A shared "engine room."** All the data — homes, bookings, users, reviews — lives in one secure, reliable database (built on Supabase, the same kind of trusted technology behind many modern apps). The phone app and the website both talk to this single source, so everyone always sees the same up-to-date information.
- **Security built into the foundation.** The system enforces who can see and do what — a guest can't see another guest's trips; a host only manages their own homes. This is part of the foundation, not bolted on later.

> Think of it like a restaurant: the **menu and dining room** (the app screens)
> are what guests see, the **kitchen** (the database) is shared and central, and
> the **staff rules** (security) make sure the right people handle the right things.

Ryo is designed to feel **calm, warm, and a little luxurious** — the opposite of a
cluttered booking site. Deliberate choices:

- **Warm cream backgrounds**, never harsh white.

The name **Ryo** (旅, "journey") and our service philosophy come from the Japanese
idea of *omotenashi* — anticipating a guest's needs before they even ask. That
spirit guides every design and product decision.

## 10. How Ryo makes money

Ryo earns the same clean way the best marketplaces do: **a small percentage of
each booking** — and nothing sneaky on top.

**The take rate.** On a typical booking Ryo keeps roughly **14–16%**, split between
guest and host and fully disclosed:

| Fee | Paid by | Typical | Shown to them? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Guest service fee | Guest | 8–11% of the stay | Yes — on the receipt, before paying |
| Host service fee | Host | 5–8% of the payout | Yes — on the host statement |
| Currency margin | Guest paying in another currency | small, ~1–1.5% | Yes — disclosed on the receipt |

> **Crucial brand rule:** the total price the guest sees in search is *exactly*
> what they pay. No surprise add-ons. And hosts **cannot pay to rank higher** —
> the take rate is our only revenue. Quality wins placement, not advertising spend.

**Holding the money safely (escrow).** When a guest pays, Ryo *holds* the money
until **24 hours after check-in**, then releases it to the host. This is the quiet
mechanism that makes guarantees possible — if a home fails on arrival, we can make
the guest whole immediately, without a payment fight.

**A simple worked example** (illustrative, not committed):

- A guest books a home at **~$280/night for 4 nights ≈ $1,120**.
- Ryo's blended take (~15%) ≈ **$168 of revenue** on that one booking.
- After the cost of running the concierge, payment processing, and the guarantee
  reserve, roughly **half of that revenue** is profit contribution per booking.

Multiply that across thousands of high-quality bookings and you have a business
with healthy margins — *because* we focus on premium, curated stays rather than
chasing the cheapest, highest-volume listings.

## 11. The market opportunity

- The **global short-term-rental market** is worth **$120 billion+ a year** and still growing.
- **India and Southeast Asia** are growing fastest off a smaller base — younger travellers, more weekend-trip behaviour, and *lower trust* in existing platforms (which is exactly the gap Ryo closes).
- Our specific wedge — **premium, curated stays** — is a smaller slice by count but has much higher value per booking and far better economics.

We deliberately launch where the economics are best (India + SEA) and expand
outward as our supply of quality homes grows.

## 12. Why this beats Airbnb on its own ground

We're not trying to out-scale Airbnb. We compete on the one thing scale can't
deliver: **consistent service**.

| | Airbnb | Ryo |
|---|---|---|
| Listings | Endless | Curated; the best surfaced first |
| Host verification | Self-declared | Real ID + proof of property |
| Support | Hours/days, scripted | ~2 min, 24/7, in the guest's language |
| Pricing | Fees added at checkout | Total price shown up front |
| If a stay fails | Refund argument | Re-book or make-whole guarantee |
| Posture | "We're a marketplace" | "We stand behind every stay" |

**Result for the guest:** a booking they don't have to babysit.
**Result for the host:** a platform that backs them and protects their name.

## 14. The plan — from "built" to "launched" to "growing"

Having the app built is the *starting line*, not the finish. The plan has four
clear stages — like turning a finished showroom car into one that's actually on
the road carrying passengers.

### Stage 1 — Switch on the real engine *(a few weeks)*
Turn the core systems from "demo" to live:

- **Real accounts & sign-in** that work for anyone, anywhere.
- **Real money** — connect payment providers (Stripe + Razorpay for India) so guests pay and hosts get paid; money held safely until 24h after check-in.
- **Real emails** — confirmations, receipts, notifications.
- **A web address** — secure the domains (e.g. ryostays.com) and put the site live on the internet.
- **The legal basics** — Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, cookie consent.

### Stage 2 — Make every button truly work *(several weeks)*
Wire up every action behind the screens:

- **Hosts** manage calendars, accept/decline bookings, set up payouts, reply to reviews, message guests.
- **Our team** can suspend bad actors, approve or reject listings, issue refunds, resolve incidents — every action recorded.
- **Guests** get the finishing touches: write reviews, message hosts before booking, named wishlists, full trip details with check-in instructions.

### Stage 3 — Polish, safety & App Stores *(a few weeks, overlaps Stage 2)*
- **Multiple languages & currencies** properly switched on.
- **Safety & support** — a real "report a problem" path and our published promise (*answer within 2 minutes on chat*).
- **Reliability** — monitoring so we know instantly if something breaks, plus speed and security tuning.
- **Phone apps published** to the Apple App Store and Google Play (these carry a ~2-week review wait, so we start early).

### Stage 4 — Launch & grow *(ongoing)*
Open the doors deliberately, not all at once:

1. **Soft launch** — invite a small group (friends, family, a few curated hosts) to a private version, and catch problems while the audience is small and forgiving.
2. **Curated launch in India + SEA** — hand-pick ~200–500 excellent homes. Quality from day one; we'd rather have 300 great stays than 30,000 random ones. *This curation is our advantage — the big players can't copy it without hurting their own brand.*
3. **Turn on the concierge** — the 24/7 multilingual human support that is the heart of Ryo. Service quality *becomes* our marketing.
4. **Grow on proof** — only once guests start re-booking (the real sign they loved it) do we spend on advertising and open up to more hosts and nearby countries.

> **The honest takeaway:** the hard, expensive part — designing and building the
> whole app — is largely *done*. What remains is mostly *connecting* it to live
> systems and *opening it carefully*. That's a faster, lower-risk phase than what's
> already behind us.

## 15. Timeline & what we need from partners

**Realistic timeline to public launch:** roughly **7–9 weeks of focused work**
with a small team (including unavoidable waits like App Store review, domain
setup, and legal). With more hands it compresses to **4–5 weeks**. Growth (Stage
4) then continues for as long as the business runs.

**A few things move faster with help from the business/partner side**, because
they have waiting periods worth starting early:

- Registering the **domain names**.
- Opening **Apple and Google developer accounts** (needed to publish the phone apps).
- Choosing an **email provider** and a **legal-policy service** (for Terms/Privacy).
- Lining up the **first hand-picked hosts** for the curated launch.
- Planning the **concierge team** (who answers guests 24/7) ahead of Stage 4.

None of these are large — but starting them in parallel keeps the launch on the
faster end of the timeline.

## 16. Risks & how we hold them

Being honest about what could go wrong, and how we've designed around it:

| Risk | How we hold it |
|---|---|
| Concierge costs grow faster than bookings | Tier the guarantee (premium vs standard), and use smart automation for routine questions — keep humans for the moments that matter. |
| Not enough quality homes at launch | Start tight: one focused region, hand-curated homes. We'd rather grow slowly than lower the bar. |
| City rules on short-term rentals | Detect location, warn hosts at listing time, cooperate with city authorities, and design for compliance from day one. |
| "Premium" being read as "expensive" | Lead with *honest total pricing* as the brand cue — not exclusivity. |
| A serious trust incident | Trust & safety is a core product, not a policy page: verification + held funds + an insurance partner + a full audit trail bound the worst case. |

---

## 17. Why Ryo is hard to copy — our moat

1. **Service operations.** *"Just Ryo it."* is backed by a real, SLA-driven concierge function. A competitor can't bolt genuine 24/7 multilingual hospitality onto a scale model overnight.
2. **Curation discipline.** Refusing low-quality supply costs us short-term volume but builds a quality reputation the volume players can't match without diluting their own brand.
3. **Trust built into the money flow.** Verification, held funds, and guarantees are part of how the system works — not promises on a help page.
4. **Global by design.** Multiple languages, currencies, and region rules are foundational, so cross-border travel — the natural shape of Ryo — works from day one. Expanding is an operational step, not a rebuild.
